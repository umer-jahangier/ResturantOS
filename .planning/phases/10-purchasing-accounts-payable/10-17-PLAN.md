## THE DECISION (2026-07-13) — decision id 10-17-A

**AR is IN scope for FIN-05. It is not descoped.**

**Source of receivables: corporate / house accounts.** Restaurants bill corporate clients, catering
customers, and regulars *on account*; the customer settles later. That is a real receivable with a real
writer. The original "nothing in the product generates a receivable" analysis was wrong only because the
house-account feature had never been written down — not because the need was absent.

**The hard constraint that shapes the split: POS DOES NOT EXIST YET.** Phase 7 (Point of Sale & Kitchen
Display) is 0/4 plans, Not started. The "charge to account" *tender type* — the thing that turns an order
close into a receivable — physically cannot be wired into POS during Phase 10. Building an AR ledger with
no writer would produce precisely the orphaned, structurally-always-empty sub-ledger the audit warned
about.

**Therefore AR is split across two phases:**

| Phase | Owns | Status |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Phase 10 (plan 10-18)** | The AR sub-ledger + the house/corporate customer-account entity + AR balances + AR aging + a real, tested **internal seam** (`POST /internal/finance/ar/charges`) that Phase 7's POS will call on order close. AR is exercisable NOW via the internal seam and via a manual "post a charge to a house account" path — it is not merely defined. | This phase |
| **Phase 7 (follow-up)** | The POS **"charge to account" tender type**, which on order close calls the Phase 10 seam with the order's `customerId` + total. | Deferred, tracked |

Phase 7's SC#4 already says `ORDER_CLOSED` is published carrying `customerId` — the identifier the seam
needs is already in Phase 7's own success criteria. The seam is designed to be consumed, not invented later.

**Why not OPA-gate AR:** a credit limit is a domain invariant on the customer account, not an approval
workflow. No new OPA action verb is introduced. (Stated so a future reader does not re-litigate it.)
</context>
