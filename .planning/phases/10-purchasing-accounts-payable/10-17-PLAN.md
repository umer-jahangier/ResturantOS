  <options>
    <option id="descope-ar">
      <name>Descope AR from FIN-05 (RECOMMENDED)</name>
      <pros>
        - Nothing in the product generates receivables today (POS settles at point of sale; no credit
          terms, no customer invoicing, no house accounts in any requirement). An AR sub-ledger would have
          zero writers.
        - AR balances ARE already queryable: account `1200 Accounts Receivable` is seeded in the Pakistan
          CoA and readable via the existing `GET /api/v1/finance/gl/balances`. Any journal entry that ever
          credits/debits it is tracked. The ledger is correct; only a dedicated sub-ledger report is absent.
        - Zero new code, zero new attack surface, zero new migration.
        - Honest documentation beats a hollow feature: the failure this phase is being reopened for is
          requirements being marked Complete on the strength of argument. Writing down "AR is out of scope
          until a credit-sales feature exists" is the opposite of that.
      </pros>
      <cons>
        - FIN-05's text changes. A future credit-sales / B2B-catering feature will need a real AR
          sub-ledger and an aging report, and will have to reopen this.
        - "AR is tracked via the generic GL endpoint" is a weaker claim than the dedicated AP aging report,
          and the asymmetry will look odd to a reader who doesn't know why.
      </cons>
    </option>
    <option id="implement-ar">
      <name>Implement an AR sub-ledger + aging report now</name>
      <pros>
        - FIN-05's text is satisfied literally; AP and AR are symmetrical.
        - The scaffolding is ready when a credit-sales feature lands.
      </pros>
      <cons>
        - It has no writers. Every bucket renders 0.00 forever until a feature that creates receivables
          exists — a report that structurally cannot show data is worse than no report, because it looks
          like it works.
        - It expands Phase 10, which is already reopened with 4 blockers, with speculative work.
        - The right design for AR depends entirely on the credit-sales model (terms, dunning, customer
          entity) that has not been specified. Building it now means guessing, then rebuilding.
      </cons>
    </option>
