<objective>
Gate all 18 public purchasing endpoints with RBAC, and seed the permissions they (and `vendor.rego`) depend on.

Purpose: `PurchasingSecurityConfig` declares `@EnableMethodSecurity` and then no controller uses it — every route is `anyRequest().authenticated()`. Today a Cashier can create vendors AND their bank details, approve POs, override a 3-way match, post AP payments, and record goods receipts. That is a straight authorization hole under ROADMAP SC#1 ("Managers manage vendors") and SC#2/#3. Meanwhile `vendor.manage` and `vendor.po.approve` sit in the auth seed with ZERO consumers, and `vendor.po.close` (called by `PurchaseOrderService.close`) is not seeded as a permission at all.

Output: `@PreAuthorize` on every public purchasing handler, a complete permission seed + role grants, and an IT that proves a Cashier is rejected and a Manager is not — through the real Spring Security filter chain, not a mock.
</objective>
