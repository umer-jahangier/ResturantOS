<objective>
Close the AR persona gap, and settle the TOTP catch-22 with an explicit recommendation.

**The persona gap.** Plan 10-18 shipped Accounts Receivable (house/corporate accounts: create, charge,
settle) and its core write path **has never been exercised by a human**. No usable seeded account holds
`finance.ar.manage`. Verified against the seed data: `finance.ar.manage` is granted to OWNER, TENANT_ADMIN
and ACCOUNTANT only; MANAGER gets `finance.ar.view` alone (confirmed in UAT: manager1
`GET .../ar/customer-accounts` → 200, `POST` → 403). And every one of those three roles trips
`AuthServiceImpl.requiresTotpStepUp()` — ACCOUNTANT holds `finance.period.close`; OWNER/TENANT_ADMIN hold
`rbac.manage` — so all three are TOTP-locked with no enrolled secret. 10-18's deliverable is therefore
unverified in the real world.

**The TOTP catch-22 — RECOMMENDATION: DEFER the product fix to Phase 2, unblock Phase 10 here.**
`requiresTotpStepUp()` forces step-up for any holder of `rbac.manage` or `finance.period.close`, but
enrolment (`POST /api/v1/auth/2fa/setup`) requires an already-authenticated session. Any real tenant that
promotes a user to those permissions before they enrol 2FA locks them out identically. This IS a real product
bug — but fixing it properly requires a new partially-authenticated, enrolment-only token state in the auth
domain, with re-verification of every auth path. That is Phase 2 (auth) scope with a Phase-2 blast radius,
and doing it inside a purchasing gap-closure wave would be the same over-reach that produced this mess.
So: record it formally as a Phase-2 gap item (Task 3), and unblock Phase 10 now with a persona that has no
reason to need step-up at all.

Purpose: make 10-18's AR write path actually drivable, and actually drive it.
Output: an AR-capable seeded persona, a browser-proven create→charge→settle journey, and a formally filed
Phase-2 gap item for the TOTP lockout.
</objective>
